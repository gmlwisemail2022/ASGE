Types of Investment Funds on Offer

  1. Open-ended Fund Company(OFC)

    This structure is set up as a company, allowing flexibility for investors to trade the funds through the creation and cancellation of shares.

    The OFC is a separate legal entity with its own board of directors and can contract and hold assets in its own name. It is regulated by the SFC while the Registrar of Companies oversees the incorporation and statutory corporate filings of OFCs.

  2. Limited Partnership Funds (LPF)

    This structure is designed to encourage private investment funds, including PE and VC funds, to establish and register in Hong Kong, facilitating the flow of capital into start-ups in the innovation and technology field in the Greater Bay Area.

    It is structured in the form of a limited partnership, used for the purpose of managing investments for the benefit of its investors. It must be constituted by one general partner who has unlimited liability in respect of the debts and liabilities of the fund, and at least one limited partner with limited liability.

  3. Unit Trusts

    Unit trusts offer a flexible and transparent investment structure, ideal for collective investment schemes. They are managed by a trustee and a fund manager, providing investors with diversified and professionally managed portfolios.
