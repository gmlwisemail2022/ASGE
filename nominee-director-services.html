How does it work?

A nominee director is like a trusted proxy.

The nominee director serves as a stand-in to protect your identity as a director from the public. The appointed director holds an administrative position within your company, acting at the behest of the ultimate beneficial owner.

Their responsibilities may include assisting in opening corporate bank accounts, participating in company agreements and signing corporate documents. Furthermore they are accountable to follow the legal and regulatory requirements imposed by the authorities where the entity is located and also acts as a liaison between the company and various government authorities.

This helps you focus on the strategic direction of your company, while they handle the administrative details.



Our Process

Step 1: The client enters into a nominee service agreement with Asia SGE.

Step 2: The name of the appointed nominee director will appear in the public records as the director of the company.

Step 3: The nominee director will carry out his duties by acting upon your instructions.
